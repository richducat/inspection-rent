Step 02 — Set up the job

Add the property and pick the inspection type

Drop in tomorrow's booking, then tell the app what kind of inspection this is — that choice decides which official forms and photos you'll need.

You do

Create the property and fill the booking three ways: paste it from your Google Calendar, import an .ics file, or type it. Set who's on the report once — your license, initials, signature and logo — and it rides on every job after.

03

Step 03 — Research

One tap fills in the public record

Owner, parcel, year built, square footage, flood zone and permit history — pulled from official county and state sources, each with a link back to where it came from (design wind speed too, where the county GIS publishes it).

You do

Tap Research + autofill (it also runs on its own when you pick an address). Anything you already typed is never overwritten — it's kept aside for you to compare.

What comes back — the app handles all of these

Straight through

Records found

The fields fill in automatically and each source is listed with a verification link. Review, adjust, move on.

Straight through

Already pulled this address

Loads instantly from your saved copy — no waiting on the server. Hit Refresh records any time you want the latest permits.

Heads up

Nothing on file

No public record for this address? You get the official source links to check by hand, or just enter the details yourself.

Heads up

Server waking up

If the records server is slow to wake, it tells you to try again in a moment and offers the manual links — it never just spins.

04

Step 04 — Walk the property

Fill the fields and capture the photos

Plain dropdowns for roof, electrical, HVAC and plumbing — the app turns your picks into the correct boxes on the state form. Labeled photo slots make sure nothing gets forgotten in the driveway.

You do

Work the guided fields, then snap each photo into its slot — take them one at a time, or import a batch and the app auto-sorts them into the right boxes. Rotate, crop or brighten any shot right there.

Optional shortcuts & what they do

Optional

Scan a data plate

Point it at an AC, water-heater or panel label — it reads the serial, works out the age, and drops it in as a suggestion for you to confirm.

Heads up

Couldn't read it

If the label is too dark or blurry, it asks for a closer, glare-free shot rather than guessing. Nothing is ever invented.

Optional

Flag a defect

Mark a defect photo and add a recommendation — it prints in its own section and pairs to the right 4-Point system.

05

Step 05 — AI drafts, you decide

Optional photo analysis for the wind mitigation

On wind-mit jobs you can opt in to AI photo analysis. It reviews your roof-to-wall, aerial and elevation photos and drafts the trickiest form answers — Q6 roof-to-wall, Q7 roof geometry, Q9 opening protection.

You do

Tick "Use AI photo analysis" (it adds a few minutes) and run it. Every draft lands in your review queue marked Needs review, with a confidence score and the reasoning behind it. You approve, edit, or reject — nothing is ever filed on its own.

What it gives you back

Straight through

A drafted answer

When the photos clearly support one category, it suggests it with its confidence — ready for you to approve into the form.

Heads up

Not enough to be sure

If a photo is ambiguous or missing, it says so and tells you what to add — on purpose. The form asks for the weakest connection, so it would rather flag it than over-credit and hand a carrier a reason to kick it back.

06

Step 06 — Review, sign & deliver

Approve everything, sign, and hand it over

A readiness gauge tells you the moment the report is complete. Sign it, and the finished documents are one tap from your client.

You do

Work the "what's left" checklist, approve each finding and suggestion, then sign. Edit anything afterward and the report reopens for a fresh signature — so a signed report is always exactly what you approved.

Your file copy

Kept for your records

A full permit history and a complete backup stay in your files — labeled as your copy, never mixed into the client's report.

Always true — the one rule that never bends

The AI assists. A licensed inspector reviews and signs every report.

Nothing the app suggests — from a records pull to a photo scan to a wind-mit draft — is ever applied on its own or filed automatically. Every suggestion waits in a review queue, carries its confidence and reasoning, and only becomes part of the report when you approve it and sign. When the AI isn't sure, it says so and asks for more, instead of guessing. That's what keeps your name on solid ground and your reports out of the kickback pile.
